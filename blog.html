Flooding unlikely, but CLOCA warns rainy weather, warm spell could lead to 'extremely dangerous' waterways

The Central Lake Ontario Conservation Authority (CLOCA) has issued a warning urging caution in various areas due to unsettled weather conditions and anticipated warmer temperatures. Between January 24 and 27, temperatures are expected to rise above freezing with estimated precipitation of 25 to 45 mm, primarily in the form of rain. This, coupled with recent snow survey data indicating an average snow-water equivalent of 27 mm, is likely to lead to increased runoff, resulting in elevated water levels and flows in streams, according to CLOCA.


While widespread flooding is not expected, the conservation authority cautions that fast-flowing water and flooding might occur in flood-prone areas. The combination of milder temperatures and rain may also lead to the weakening and breakup of existing ice cover, causing localized water-level increases, ice jams, and flooding.


As a precautionary measure, CLOCA is advising the public to avoid watercourses, shorelines, and certain structures like bridges, culverts, and dams. The dangerous conditions along stream banks and Lake Ontario shorelines, including increased wave activity and slippery surfaces, make these locations extremely hazardous.


CLOCA has declared that its water safety statement will remain in effect through January 28 or until further notice. Authorities are actively monitoring the situation, and residents are encouraged to stay informed about updates and exercise caution in the mentioned areas.


No injuries after gunshot goes through patio door at Pickering apartment

Durham Regional Police are actively investigating an incident where a gunshot was fired at a Pickering apartment near Pickering Parkway and Valley Farm Road around 9 p.m. on Wednesday, Jan. 17. The victim's apartment was targeted, as the gunshot went through a patio door; however, the incident does not seem to be a deliberate act. Fortunately, no injuries were reported.


Authorities are urging the public's assistance and requesting surveillance and dash camera footage to aid in the ongoing investigation. Individuals with relevant information are encouraged to contact Detective Constable Street at 1-888-579-1520, extension 2074.


Residents are reminded to remain vigilant, and any information provided could contribute to resolving the case.
